# Gauge and Instrument Display Research Atlas

**Version:** 0.1 — 2026-07-15  
**Coverage:** 136 instrument/display families, 885 separately stated behaviours  
**Companion data:** `gauge_display_research_catalog.json`

## Executive summary

This is a mechanism-first catalogue for building gauge visualisations that behave like instruments rather than decorative circles with opinions. It runs from liquid columns, elastic capsules and clockwork recorders through moving-coil meters, flip-dots, Nixies, VFDs, segmented LCDs, CRTs and specialist composite instruments.

A literally exhaustive list is impossible: instrument makers combined movements, scales, contacts, lamps, pointers and housings in thousands of custom permutations. This atlas instead aims for **mechanism coverage**. If the renderer can reproduce every mechanism and behaviour listed here, most named instruments become data, artwork and parameter presets rather than new engine code.

The central design recommendation is to keep five layers separate:

1. **Measurement/transducer state** — what the physical sensor would actually know.
2. **Signal conditioning** — filtering, calibration, sample rate, deadband, range switching and failure state.
3. **Mechanical or electrical movement** — inertia, damping, friction, backlash, stops, stepping and memory pointers.
4. **Optical/display mechanism** — emitted or reflected light, scan pattern, persistence, viewing angle and parallax.
5. **Age, environment and faults** — temperature, vibration, supply droop, wear, dirt, faded paint and failed segments.

Do not drive rendered needle angle directly from an MQTT value. Drive a simulated instrument state, then render that state. Otherwise the result knows the number but has never met a gauge.

## 1. Scope and boundaries

**Included:** direct-reading physical gauges; mechanical and electromechanical indicators; segmented, vector, bistable and other displays whose physical mechanism gives them a recognisable behaviour; CRT/HUD technologies where persistence, optics or scanning matter.

**Excluded:** a generic TFT/OLED/HDMI framebuffer whose only relevant behaviour is “show these pixels”. A segmented OLED is included; an arbitrary 4K dashboard is not. The latter is perfectly useful, but so is plywood, and neither is automatically an instrument.

**Era notation:** the date is an approximate period of documented or common use, not a claim that every maker adopted or abandoned the mechanism on New Year’s Day. `present` means examples are still manufactured or used somewhere, not that they remain dominant.

**Date confidence:** `H` = supported by a dated primary, museum or official source; `M` = approximate adoption/common-use range assembled from reliable overviews; `L` would mean especially uncertain or region-specific. No current entry relies on `L`.

## 2. A practical realism model

### 2.1 Scale and geometry

- Linear, logarithmic, square-root, reciprocal and piecewise scales.
- Centre-zero, suppressed zero, elevated zero, reversed direction and multiple revolutions.
- Dual units, overlapping scales, coloured operating arcs, redlines and adjustable reference bugs.
- Pointer length, thickness, counterweight, shadow height, lens curvature and observer parallax.

### 2.2 Dynamics

A useful default pointer model is a second-order system, then made imperfect:

`angle_ddot + 2*zeta*omega*angle_dot + omega^2*(angle - target) = 0`

Add Coulomb friction/stiction, backlash, quantisation, hard stops and a small stop restitution coefficient. Different families then become presets: a glycerine-filled Bourdon gauge has high damping; a cheap cable speedometer has low-frequency cable whip; a chronometric tachometer updates in timed steps; a VU meter has specified ballistic averaging.

### 2.3 Optical and electrical behaviour

- Multiplex duty cycle, scan order, inter-digit blanking, ghosting and camera-visible flicker.
- Segment-current regulation, shared-current budgets and supply impedance.
- Lamp warm-up, phosphor persistence, bloom, burn-in, filament gradients and viewing-angle contrast.
- Reflective versus emissive operation, ambient-light washout, filters, diffusers and internal reflections.

A useful correction to a common assumption: **more lit LED segments do not inherently have to be dimmer**. That behaviour appears when the original circuit used a common resistor, weak supply, multiplex current limit or shared driver budget. It is an excellent optional fault/period-circuit preset, not a universal law of seven-segment LEDs.

### 2.4 Memory, controls and failure states

- Drag pointers, max/min witnesses, latching flags, chart traces and bistable displays remember history.
- Reset knobs, caging controls, set-point bugs, lamp tests, zero adjusters and startup sweeps are part of the instrument.
- Model open circuits, stuck mechanisms, partial power, invalid flags, overrange, sensor disconnect and frozen last value.

### 2.5 Restraint

Realism is usually subtle. If every pointer bounces, every lamp flickers and every Nixie misfires at once, the panel stops looking old and starts looking haunted. Faults should be parameters, not confetti.

## 7. Suggested software data model

The companion JSON is a research catalogue, not yet a rendering schema. A practical runtime preset can resemble this:

```json
{
  "id": "P04",
  "family": "bourdon_pressure_gauge",
  "measurement": {
    "mqtt_topic": "plant/boiler/pressure_kpa",
    "unit": "kPa",
    "valid_range": [
      0,
      1600
    ],
    "failure_state": "sensor_open"
  },
  "scale": {
    "mapping": "piecewise_linear",
    "start_angle_deg": -135,
    "sweep_deg": 270,
    "major_divisions": 8,
    "minor_divisions": 5,
    "secondary_unit": "psi"
  },
  "movement": {
    "model": "second_order_with_friction",
    "natural_frequency_hz": 2.3,
    "damping_ratio": 0.42,
    "coulomb_friction_deg": 0.18,
    "backlash_deg": 0.35,
    "hard_stop_restitution": 0.12
  },
  "display": {
    "pointer_parallax_mm": 3.0,
    "lens_distortion": 0.04,
    "backlight": "none"
  },
  "environment": {
    "temperature_c": 22,
    "vibration_rms": 0.0,
    "supply_v": 12.0
  },
  "age_faults": {
    "zero_offset": 0.006,
    "paint_fade": 0.08,
    "dust": 0.03,
    "stuck": false
  }
}
```

Useful reusable enums include:

- `scale.mapping`: linear, log, sqrt, reciprocal, piecewise, lookup-table.
- `movement.model`: first-order, second-order, thermal, stepper, servo, ballistic, bistable, fluid-column.
- `memory.mode`: none, max-drag, min-drag, min-max, latching, chart, retained-pixel.
- `display.technology`: painted, incandescent, neon, filament, VFD, LED, LCD, plasma, EL, e-paper, OLED, CRT, projected.
- `fault.mode`: open, short, frozen, biased, noisy, intermittent, overrange, invalid-flag, partial-power.

For reproducible wear, seed random defects from the instrument serial number. A dead segment that migrates every page load is not ageing; it is indecision.

## 8. Implementation order

1. **Generic analogue movement engine:** arbitrary scale map, second-order dynamics, stiction, backlash, stops, pointer/lens/parallax, redlines and witness pointers.
2. **Segmented emissive engine:** LED, VFD, Nixie/Numitron approximations, multiplexing, current budget, persistence, warm-up and per-segment ageing.
3. **Mechanical state displays:** drum, tape, flip-dot, split-flap, flags, stepper homing and latching.
4. **Direct-fluid instruments:** manometer, sight glass, rotameter, thermometers, bubbles and magnetic level flags.
5. **Special composites:** altimeters, gyro instruments, chart recorders, sonar flashers and engine telegraphs.

That order maximises reuse. Starting with a lovingly exact Tempest Prognosticator would be memorable, but it also requires a leech API, which MQTT has so far had the good sense not to standardise.

## 9. Image and date notes

- Every row includes a Wikimedia Commons image-search link using the most specific useful search phrase. Open the file page to inspect author, licence and attribution requirements before reuse.
- Museum and manufacturer source pages often contain excellent reference photography, but their reuse terms vary. Treat them as research references unless the page explicitly grants a licence.
- Product names identify representative construction, not endorsements and not a claim that every model behaves identically.
- Dates should be refined per named product when you turn a family into a shipping preset. The mechanism-level ranges here are deliberately broader.
